cf-1282B2(mind)

B2. K for the Price of One (Hard Version)

原地址

思路：

  1. 大佬思路：
    If you sort the array by costs, it will always be profitable to take segments of length k with the cheapest possible end.
    It remains only to understand when you need to take gifts without a promotion.
    It makes no sense to take k or more gifts without a promotion, so we can combine them and buy together.
    It also makes no sense to take not the cheapest gifts without a stock, since the total cost from this will only increase.
    So the solution to the problem is to iterate over the prefix in a sorted array of length no more than k,
    and then buy items together for k pieces. This solution works in the linear time
    since we will always look at elements with a different index by modulo k.
